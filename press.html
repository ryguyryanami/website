Ryguy Rynami

About

Based out of his home studio in Long Beach, California, Ryguy Ryanami is the moniker of multi-instrumentalist and producer Ryan Burke—a sonic architect who bridges the gap between technical precision and kaleidoscopic indie pop. His sound is a vibrant, "semi-psychedelic" exploration of melody, merging Animal Collective’s experimental quirk with the soaring, sun-drenched energy of MGMT and Tame Impala.

Ryan’s journey into sound didn't begin with a traditional garage band, but through the rigorous lens of engineering and experimental design. A 2014 graduate of UC Berkeley, he spent his formative years at the Center for New Music and Audio Technology (CNMAT), where he obsessed over the intersection of movement and synthesis. This era saw him building his own digital instruments—from mobile apps that turned accelerometers into percussion to Xbox Kinect programs that translated human motion into MIDI.

By 2022, this fascination with "computer music" evolved into a focused production practice within Ableton Live. Today, Ryguy Ryanami operates as a true DIY powerhouse: he writes, performs every instrument, tracks vocals, and handles the entire mixing and mastering process from his home studio in Long Beach. This end-to-end control allows for a sound that is fiercely singular—a brand of "experimental indie pop" that feels both meticulously engineered and joyfully spontaneous.

When he isn’t shaping soundscapes, Burke shapes the next generation of technical minds as an AP Computer Science and Cybersecurity educator at the historic Long Beach Polytechnic High School, where he also founded the school’s esports program. This balance of logic and lyricism defines the Ryguy Ryanami project: a blend of high-concept digital textures and human, "everything is gonna be alright" optimism.

Following the momentum of his debut EP in late 2025, Ryguy Ryanami is set to release his first full-length album, Everything is gonna be alright!, on March 21, 2026. The record serves as a definitive statement of his "uplifting-yet-unpredictable" aesthetic—a collection of songs that refuse to fit into a single box, much like the artist himself.
